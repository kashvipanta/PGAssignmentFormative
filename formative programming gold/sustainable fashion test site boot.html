The fashion industry is responsible for 8% of carbon emissions

Trying to dress sustainbly not only helps the planet but also have several other benefits:

  • Helps workers avoid being exploited by sweatshops
  • Gives you high quality clothing lasting much longer and saving money in replacements
  • Encourages you to develop your own personal style instead of being influenced by trends

TIPS AND TRICKS



Sustainable fashion can sometimes seem pretty pricey but there are several basic tips that can easily help you dress both consciously and economically.

Invest in timeless pieces

Trends are coming and going faster and faster, making it expensive to keep up with them. However some things never go out of style. 'Timeless' clothes such as basics (eg. plain t shirts and jeans or knitwear in the winter) will always look good and can also last you several years so investing in these is always a good option. And that's not to say outfits will be boring! These act as a brilliant base layer to add your own personal flair by adding on accessories and jewellery to customize it and let your personality shine through. And although these are good to make up the majority of your wardrobe with, a few statement pieces here and there would be great too.

Maintain what you own

In our busy day to day lives, it's easy to just buy new clothes instead of looking after the ones we already have, but it doesn't have to be time consuming! Something as simple as reading the label on a garment and not putting it in the dryer when instructed not to can make clothes last for so much longer. For certain clothes, hanging them on hangers or folding them properly instead of shoving them in the wardrobe will reduce damage and creases while shopping sustainably will help clear out cluttered wardrobe space to make this possible
